Urban Biome: Empowering Urban Planners for Greener Cities

"Urban Biome" is an innovative project aimed at addressing the challenges urban planners face in identifying optimal locations for green spaces in cities. It combines environmental analysis, community input, and advanced technologies to create data-driven solutions for sustainable urban development. The Urban Biome project leverages a robust tech stack to deliver powerful insights and visualization capabilities. GIS data is used for spatial analysis, enabling precise mapping of urban regions and green spaces. The project employs Leaflet.js, a lightweight JavaScript library, to create interactive and user-friendly maps that allow planners to explore environmental data dynamically. Machine learning, specifically the Random Forest algorithm, is utilized to evaluate critical environmental factors such as soil quality, elevation, and air quality, generating a comprehensive "Green Score" for each location. This analysis is supported by a cloud-based infrastructure, ensuring scalability, real-time data access, and seamless integration of additional datasets or cities. Together, these technologies form a cohesive system that empowers urban planners to make data-driven decisions while fostering sustainable development.
